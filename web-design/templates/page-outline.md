## Page-specific notes

### About page
- Lead with the *why*, not the resume. What problem made you start this?
- Include a real photo of the human(s).
- Bio < 250 words. Save the longer version for a separate "Press" or "CV" page.
- End with a CTA (most likely: "Get in touch" or "See our work").

### Services / Products page
- One section per service/product. Same template each: name, who it's for, what it includes, price (or starting price / "from"), CTA.
- If pricing isn't public, say *why* ("every project is scoped individually") so the visitor doesn't feel hidden.

### Contact page
- Keep it minimal. Form > contact info > nothing else.
- Form fields: name, email, message. Add phone/company only if you actually need them — every extra field reduces submissions.
- Confirm success in a friendly tone. Tell them when to expect a reply.

### Blog post
- H1 = post title. H2s = scannable section headers.
- First paragraph: tell the reader what they'll learn and why it matters to them.
- Short paragraphs (2–4 sentences). Bullet lists where natural.
- End with a CTA related to the post topic, not a generic one.
