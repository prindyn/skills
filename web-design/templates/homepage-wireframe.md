# Homepage Wireframe — [Project Name]

> Section-by-section outline of the homepage. Each section has one job. If a section can't justify its existence against the primary CTA, cut it.

## Section 1 — Hero (above the fold)

**Goal:** In 5 seconds, the visitor understands who you are, what you offer, and what to do next.

- **Headline:** [one short, specific sentence — what you do *for whom*]
  > e.g., "We build websites for restaurants that book more reservations."
- **Subhead:** [one sentence on the differentiator or proof]
  > e.g., "Used by 200+ independent restaurants since 2021."
- **Primary CTA button:** [action verb + outcome, e.g., "Book a free demo"]
- **Visual:** [hero image, video, or animation — single focal point, on-brand]

## Section 2 — Problem / Empathy

**Goal:** Show you understand the visitor's situation. They feel seen.

- One short paragraph or 3 bullets naming the visitor's pain.
- No mention of you yet — this is about them.

## Section 3 — Offer / Solution

**Goal:** Present what you do as the solution to that problem.

- 3 features or services, each with: icon/image, short title, 1-sentence benefit.
- Benefits, not specs. ("Get more bookings" not "Integrated reservation widget.")

## Section 4 — Proof / Social validation

**Goal:** Reduce risk. Make the visitor believe it actually works.

- 1–3 testimonials (with name, photo, role/business if possible).
- Logos of recognizable clients / press / partners.
- A specific number if you have one ("Booked 12,000+ reservations last quarter").

## Section 5 — How it works (optional)

**Goal:** Demystify the process. Make the next step feel small.

- 3 steps. Each one verb-led. ("1. Book a call. 2. We design. 3. You launch.")

## Section 6 — Secondary CTA / About teaser (optional)

**Goal:** For visitors not yet ready for the primary CTA, give them a way to keep exploring.

- Short blurb + link to About or Blog.

## Section 7 — Final CTA

**Goal:** Catch the visitor who scrolled all the way down. Same CTA as the hero, restated.

- Bold headline restating the value.
- The same primary CTA button.

## Footer

- Contact info, social links, legal links, newsletter signup (if applicable).

## Design notes

- Visual hierarchy: hero headline = largest text on the page. Primary CTA button = the only element in your accent color.
- Whitespace generously between sections. Don't crowd.
- Test on mobile first — the hero should still land in 5 seconds on a phone.
